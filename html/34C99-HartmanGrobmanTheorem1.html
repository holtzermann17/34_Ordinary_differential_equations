Hartman-Grobman theorem


Consider the differential equation

x′=f⁢(x) (1)

where f is a C1 vector field. Assume that x0 is a hyperbolic equilibrium of f. Denote Φt⁢(x) the flow of (1) through x at time t. Then there exists a homeomorphism φ⁢(x)=x+h⁢(x) with h bouded, such that

φ∘et⁢D⁢f⁢(x0)=Φt∘φ

is a sufficiently small neighboorhood of x0.

This fundamental theorem in the qualitative analysis of nonlinear differential equations states that, in a small neighborhood of x0, the flow of the nonlinear equation (1) is qualitatively similar to that of the linear system x′=D⁢f⁢(x0)⁢x.
