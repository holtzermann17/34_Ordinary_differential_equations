Picard’s theorem


Theorem 1 (Picard’s theorem [KF]).

Let E be an open subset of R2 and a continuous function f⁢(x,y) defined as f:E→R. If (x0,y0)∈E and f satisfies the Lipschitz condition in the variable y in E:

|f⁢(x,y)-f⁢(x,y1)|≤M⁢|y-y1|

where M is a constant. Then the ordinary differential equation defined as

d⁢yd⁢x=f⁢(x,y)

with the initial condition

y⁢(x0)=y0

has a unique solution y⁢(x) on some interval |x-x0|≤δ.

The above theorem is also named the Picard-Lindelöf theorem and can be generalized to a system of first order ordinary differential equations

Theorem 2 (generalization of Picard’s theorem [KF]).

Let E be an open subset of Rn+1 and a continuous function f⁢(x,y1,…,yn) defined as f=(f1,…,fn):E→Rn. If (t0,y10,…,yn⁢0)∈E and f satisfies the Lipschitz condition in the variable y1,…,yn in E:

|fi⁢(x,y1,…,yn)-fi⁢(x,y1′⁢…,yn′)|≤M⁢max1≤j≤n⁡|yj-yj′|

where M is a constant. Then the system of ordinary differential equation defined as

d⁢y1d⁢x =f1⁢(x,y1,…,yn)
⋮
d⁢ynd⁢x =fn⁢(x,y1,…,yn)

with the initial condition

y1⁢(x0)=y10,…,yn⁢(x0)=yn⁢0

has a unique solution

y1⁢(x),…,yn⁢(x)

on some interval |x-x0|≤δ.
